Pradhan Mantri MUDRA Yojana (PMMY) 2026

Scheme Overview

Pradhan Mantri MUDRA Yojana (PMMY) is a flagship financial inclusion scheme launched on April 8, 2015, by Prime Minister Narendra Modi. The scheme is designed to provide loans up to ₹10 lakh (increased to ₹20 lakh in recent budget expansions) to small business owners, shopkeepers, artisans, and startup entrepreneurs who run non-corporate, non-farm small and micro enterprises.

Benefits & Financial Assistance

MUDRA (Micro Units Development & Refinance Agency) acts as a refinancing institution. The key benefit of a Mudra loan is that it is collateral-free (no security required) and has competitive interest rates with flexible repayment terms of up to 5 years. Loans are categorized into three stages based on funding requirements.

Loan Category Target Funding Limit Suitable For
Shishu Up to ₹50,000 New startups, micro-vendors, small shops
Kishore ₹50,000 to ₹5 Lakh Established businesses looking to buy equipment or inventory
Tarun ₹5 Lakh to ₹10 Lakh Expanding businesses requiring larger capital (Budget 2024 increased limit up to ₹20 Lakh under special conditions)

Eligibility Criteria

  • Any Indian citizen who has a viable business plan for a non-farm income-generating activity.
  • Activities include manufacturing, processing, trading, or service sector industries.
  • Micro and small enterprises, proprietorships, partnerships, and small-scale manufacturing units are eligible.

Exclusion List

  • Corporate bodies/large industries.
  • Farming/agricultural activities (though allied activities like poultry, dairy, and beekeeping are eligible).

Documents Required

  • Mudra Application Form: Specific to Shishu, Kishore, or Tarun categories.
  • Identity & Address Proof: Aadhaar Card, Voter ID, PAN Card, Driving License.
  • Business Proof: Registration certificate, license, tax registration, or shop establishment certificate.
  • Financial Statements: (For Kishore & Tarun) Last 2 years balance sheet, IT returns, and bank statements.

How to Apply

  1. Prepare a viable business plan outlining your activity, expenses, and projected profits.
  2. Approach any commercial bank, regional rural bank, co-operative bank, or micro-finance institution.
  3. Submit the completed Mudra application form along with the required documents.
  4. The bank will verify your creditworthiness, business viability, and process the loan.
  5. Alternatively, apply online through the Udyam Mitra portal (udyamimitra.in) or bank-specific portals.
  6. Once approved, you will receive a Mudra RuPay Card, which works like a credit/debit card to withdraw working capital.

Status Check

  1. Track your application status via the Udyam Mitra portal using your reference number.
  2. Contact your respective bank branch where you submitted the physical documents for regular tracking updates.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is a Mudra Card?

A Mudra Card is a debit card issued against the Mudra loan account for working capital requirements. It allows the entrepreneur to make transactions and withdraw cash in a flexible manner, paying interest only on the utilized amount.

Is a guarantor required for a Mudra Loan?

No. Under the Credit Guarantee Fund for Micro Units (CGFMU), no third-party guarantee or collateral is required for Mudra loans.

What is the interest rate for Mudra Loans?

Interest rates vary from bank to bank and are linked to the RBI's Marginal Cost of Funds Based Lending Rate (MCLR). Typically, it ranges between 8.5% and 12% per annum.
