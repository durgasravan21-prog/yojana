Scheme Overview

The Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (MGNREGA) is a historical labor law and social security measure enacted in September 2005. The scheme guarantees 100 days of wage employment in a financial year to rural households. It is designed to boost rural purchasing power and create durable local assets like roads, ponds, and canals.

Benefits & Financial Assistance

Rural households receive guaranteed unskilled labor employment within 15 days of applying. Wages are paid directly into bank accounts based on state-specific wage rates. If employment is not provided within 15 days, applicants are entitled to receive an unemployment allowance.

Feature Guarantee / Details
Guaranteed Employment 100 days per rural household per financial year
Wages Payment Mode Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) to bank account
Work Location Within 5 km radius of the village
Unemployment Allowance Paid if work is not given within 15 days of demand

Eligibility Criteria

  • The applicant must be a resident of a rural area in India.
  • The applicant must be an adult member of the rural household (aged 18 or above).
  • The applicant must volunteer for unskilled manual labor.

Exclusion List

  • Urban residents.
  • Minor children (aged below 18).
  • Skilled/professional work seekers (the scheme covers only unskilled manual labor).

Documents Required

  • Aadhaar Card: For biometric registration.
  • Proof of Residence: Voter ID, ration card, or gram panchayat verification.
  • Bank Account Details: Linked with Aadhaar for receiving wages.

How to Apply

  1. Approach your local Gram Panchayat office in your village.
  2. Request a 'Job Card Application Form' (Form 1).
  3. Fill in the household details and names of adult family members willing to work.
  4. Attach passport-size photos and a copy of your Aadhaar card.
  5. Submit the form to the Gram Panchayat. The Panchayat will verify details and issue an official **NREGA Job Card** within 15 days.
  6. Once you have the Job Card, submit a written request for work to the Gram Panchayat.

Status Check

  1. Visit the official NREGA portal at nrega.nic.in.
  2. Select your State, District, Block, and Gram Panchayat.
  3. Click on 'Job Card' to view the household job card details, work history, and payment status.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is the current wage rate under MGNREGA?

Wages vary by state, ranging from approximately ₹230 to ₹370 per day, adjusted annually by the Central Government based on the Consumer Price Index (CPI-AL).

How is the work allocated?

Work is allocated on local assets like water conservation projects, afforestation, rural connectivity, land development, and sanitation infrastructure within the village.

Is there a reservation for women under MGNREGA?

Yes, the Act mandates that at least one-third (33%) of the beneficiaries must be women.
