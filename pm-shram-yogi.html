Scheme Overview

Pradhan Mantri Shram Yogi Maan-Dhan (PM-SYM) is a voluntary and contributory pension scheme launched in February 2019 by the Ministry of Labour and Employment, Government of India. The scheme is aimed at securing old age social security for unorganized workers like street vendors, rickshaw pullers, brick kiln workers, and ragpickers.

Benefits & Financial Assistance

Subscribers receive a guaranteed minimum monthly pension of ₹3,000 after attaining the age of 60. The scheme is a 50:50 contributory scheme where the subscriber contributes a monthly premium, and the Central Government makes an equal matching contribution.

Entry Age Subscriber Monthly Contribution Government Matching Contribution Total Monthly Pension Fund
18 Years ₹55 ₹55 ₹110
25 Years ₹80 ₹80 ₹160
30 Years ₹105 ₹105 ₹210
40 Years (Max Age) ₹200 ₹200 ₹400

Eligibility Criteria

  • The applicant must be a resident of India.
  • The applicant must be working in the unorganized sector.
  • The entry age must be between 18 and 40 years.
  • The monthly income of the applicant must be ₹15,000 or less.

Exclusion List

  • Workers covered under organized sectors (EPFO, ESIC, or NPS).
  • Income tax payers.

Documents Required

  • Aadhaar Card: Proof of identity and age.
  • Savings Bank Account Details: Passbook copy showing IFSC for auto-debit.

How to Apply

  1. Visit the nearest Common Service Centre (CSC) or register online at maandhan.in.
  2. Log in using your mobile number and click on 'Services' -> 'Enrollment'.
  3. Enter your Aadhaar, name, and DOB to verify details.
  4. Enter your bank account number, IFSC code, and select monthly premium auto-debit authorization.
  5. The portal will generate a customized 'Shram Yogi Pension Card'.

Status Check

  1. Subscribers can check their premium payment ledger and account details online at maandhan.in using their SPAN or mobile number.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What happens if a subscriber dies before 60?

If a subscriber dies before 60, their spouse is entitled to continue the scheme by paying regular premiums, or exit and claim the accumulated fund with bank interest.

Is the pension taxable?

The pension payouts of ₹3,000 per month are subject to tax guidelines in the future, though generally exempt for low-income brackets.
