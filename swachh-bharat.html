Scheme Overview

Swachh Bharat Mission (SBM) was launched by PM Narendra Modi on October 2, 2014. SBM has two arms: SBM-Gramin and SBM-Urban. The main objective was to make India 100% Open Defecation Free (ODF) through building household toilets.

Benefits & Financial Assistance

Eligible households receive a direct financial incentive of ₹12,000 to construct an Individual Household Latrine (IHHL). The incentive is released directly to the beneficiary's bank account in installments upon verification of toilet construction phases.

Beneficiary Category Subsidy Incentive Funding Split (Center/State)
Rural Households (BPL/APL-SC/ST) ₹12,000 60:40 (Plains) | 90:10 (Hilly regions)
Urban Households (LIG/EWS) Varies by State/Municipal Corporation Shared contribution

Eligibility Criteria

  • The household must not have any existing toilet.
  • Rural households listed under BPL (Below Poverty Line) list.
  • Identified APL (Above Poverty Line) rural households belonging to SC/ST, small & marginal farmers, landless laborers, women-headed households, and physically disabled individuals.

Exclusion List

  • Households that already possess a functional toilet or have previously received a government toilet construction subsidy.

Documents Required

  • Aadhaar Card: Proof of identity.
  • Bank Account Details: Bank passbook copy showing IFSC code.
  • Photograph: Photograph of the applicant standing in front of the site before and after toilet construction.

How to Apply

  1. Visit the official SBM-Gramin portal at sbm.gov.in/sbm_dbt.
  2. Register as a citizen and log in using your registered mobile number and OTP.
  3. Click on 'New Application (IHHL)' and fill in your state, district, block, and panchayat details.
  4. Enter the applicant's name as per Aadhaar and bank details.
  5. Submit the application. A village sanitation inspector will visit your site to verify.
  6. Once approved, construct the toilet structure and upload photos of the completed toilet.

Status Check

  1. Log in to the citizen portal sbm.gov.in/sbm_dbt and click 'Track Application Status' to view sanitation inspector approvals and payment release status.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is the recommended toilet design under SBM?

The twin-pit pour-flush toilet is recommended as it is cheap, easy to build, and converts human waste into organic compost naturally.

Can urban citizens apply online for toilet subsidy?

Yes, urban citizens can apply through the SBM-Urban portal or approach their local municipal corporation office.
