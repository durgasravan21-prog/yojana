Scheme Overview

The Lakhpati Didi Scheme is an ambitious national program launched by the Ministry of Rural Development, Government of India. The primary objective is to empower women members of Self-Help Groups (SHGs) by providing them with skill development training, financial literacy, and business tools to enable them to earn a sustainable income of at least ₹1,00,000 (₹1 Lakh) per year. In the 2024 budget, the target was expanded to cover 3 crore SHG women across India.

Benefits & Financial Assistance

Women under this scheme receive free vocational training in modern sectors like LED bulb manufacturing, drone operation, tailoring, plumbing, and agriculture. They also get access to interest-free or low-interest microloans, financial literacy workshops, and direct market linkage through government e-commerce platforms (like GeM).

Eligibility Criteria

  • The applicant must be a resident woman of India.
  • The applicant must be an active member of a registered Self-Help Group (SHG) in her village/town.
  • The family annual income must be low, with a focus on economically weaker sections.

Exclusion List

  • Women who are not part of any Self-Help Group (they must first join/form an SHG).
  • Families with permanent government employment.

Documents Required

  • Aadhaar Card: Proof of identity.
  • SHG Membership Certificate/ID Card: Proving active membership.
  • Income Certificate: Proving low-income bracket.
  • Bank Account Details: Bank passbook of both the individual and the SHG.

How to Apply

  1. Join or form a Self-Help Group (SHG) in your local village or urban ward.
  2. Contact your local Anganwadi center, Gram Panchayat, or Block Development Office (BDO).
  3. Express interest in the 'Lakhpati Didi' training program during SHG weekly/monthly meetings.
  4. The Block Nodal Officer under the National Rural Livelihoods Mission (NRLM) will verify active SHGs and enroll eligible candidates.
  5. Choose your desired training sector (e.g., Drone pilot, LED repair, tailoring, mushroom farming).
  6. Upon completion of the training, you can submit a business proposal through your SHG to secure microfinance/loans from linked banks.

Status Check

  1. Enrolled SHG members can check their status through the NRLM state portals or by inquiring with the block-level Livelihood Nodal Officer.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is the 'Namo Drone Didi' scheme?

It is a sub-component of the Lakhpati Didi initiative where select SHG women are trained to fly and maintain drones for agricultural spraying and crop monitoring, earning high rental income from farmers.

Is there any direct cash transfer under Lakhpati Didi?

No, this is a capacity-building and livelihood support scheme. Benefits are provided in the form of free training, toolkits, and access to bank credits rather than direct cash hand-outs.

What is the interest rate on loans under this scheme?

Loans are provided through SHG-bank linkages, which often carry highly subsidized interest rates, and in many states, interest-subvention makes them virtually interest-free.
