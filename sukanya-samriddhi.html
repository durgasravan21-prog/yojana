Sukanya Samriddhi Account Yojana (SSY) 2026

Scheme Overview

Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY) is a small deposit scheme for the girl child, launched on January 22, 2015, by Prime Minister Narendra Modi as part of the 'Beti Bachao Beti Padhao' campaign. The scheme is designed to encourage families to save for the higher education and marriage expenses of their female children, offering one of the highest interest rates among government savings schemes.

Benefits & Financial Assistance

The scheme offers a highly competitive interest rate (currently 8.2% for 2026), compounded annually. Deposits qualify for tax deductions under Section 80C, the interest earned is tax-free, and the final maturity amount is also completely exempt from income tax (EEE status).

Feature Details
Current Interest Rate (2026) 8.2% (Compounded Annually)
Min/Max Deposit per Year ₹250 / ₹1.5 Lakh
Deposit Period 15 years from account opening
Account Maturity 21 years from account opening (or marriage after age 18)
Tax Status Exempt-Exempt-Exempt (EEE) under Section 80C

Eligibility Criteria

  • The account can be opened only by the natural or legal guardian of a girl child.
  • The girl child must be a resident Indian citizen.
  • The girl child must be under the age of 10 years at the time of account opening.
  • Only one account can be opened for a single girl child, with a maximum of two accounts per family (three in case of twins/triplets).

Exclusion List

  • Non-resident Indians (NRIs) and Overseas Citizens of India (OCIs) cannot open or maintain an SSY account.
  • Male children cannot be beneficiaries of this scheme.

Documents Required

  • Girl Child Birth Certificate: Mandatory to prove age and relation.
  • Guardian's ID Proof: Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, Voter ID, or Passport.
  • Guardian's Address Proof: Electricity bill, Aadhaar, or ration card.
  • Photographs: Passport-size photos of the guardian and the girl child.

How to Apply

  1. Visit any authorized commercial bank branch or your nearest Post Office.
  2. Ask for the 'Sukanya Samriddhi Account Opening Form'.
  3. Fill in the details of the girl child (Name, Date of Birth) and the guardian.
  4. Attach the required documents (Birth certificate, Guardian PAN/Aadhaar, Photos).
  5. Submit the form and make the initial deposit (minimum ₹250).
  6. The Post Office/Bank will issue an account passbook containing details like the girl's name, DOB, account number, and date of opening.

Status Check

  1. To check status or balance, register for Internet Banking or Mobile Banking with the respective bank or Post Office.
  2. Log in to the portal and view the linked SSY account details.
  3. The physical passbook can also be updated regularly by visiting the branch.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What happens if I fail to deposit the minimum amount?

If the minimum deposit of ₹250 per year is not paid, the account will be defaulted. It can be regularized by paying a penalty of ₹50 per year along with the minimum deposit amount for the missed years.

Can I withdraw money before maturity?

Partial withdrawal up to 50% of the account balance at the end of the preceding financial year is allowed for the girl child's higher education, after she attains the age of 18 or passes Class 10.

Does the account close automatically after 21 years?

Yes, the account matures 21 years after the date of opening. If the girl child gets married after turning 18, the account can be closed early by submitting a marriage declaration.
