Scheme Overview

Atal Pension Yojana (APY) was launched by PM Narendra Modi on May 9, 2015, targeting workers in the unorganized sector (like delivery agents, maids, gardeners, drivers, and agricultural laborers). Administered by the Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority (PFRDA), the scheme encourages workers to save voluntarily for their retirement, offering a government-backed guaranteed minimum pension.

Benefits & Financial Assistance

Subscribers receive a guaranteed minimum monthly pension of ₹1,000, ₹2,000, ₹3,000, ₹4,000, or ₹5,000 after attaining the age of 60, depending on their entry age and monthly contribution. In the event of the subscriber's death, the pension is paid to the spouse, and upon the death of both, the accumulated corpus is returned to the nominee.

Eligibility Criteria

  • The subscriber must be an Indian citizen.
  • The entry age must be between 18 and 40 years.
  • The subscriber must have an active savings bank account linked to a mobile number.

Exclusion List

  • Any citizen who is an income tax payer (effective from October 1, 2022).
  • Subscribers covered under other statutory social security schemes (like EPFO, NPS, or Coal Mines Provident Fund).

Documents Required

  • Aadhaar Card: Proof of identity.
  • Savings Bank Account Details: Mandatory for auto-debit of monthly/quarterly premiums.

How to Apply

  1. Approach the bank branch where you maintain your savings account.
  2. Ask for the 'Atal Pension Yojana Proposal Form'.
  3. Select your desired monthly pension amount (₹1,000 – ₹5,000) and premium debit frequency (Monthly, Quarterly, or Half-yearly).
  4. Provide your spouse and nominee details.
  5. Consent to the auto-debit facility from your savings account.
  6. Submit the form. The bank will issue a PRAN (Permanent Retirement Account Number) card and register your account.

Status Check

  1. Check APY transaction status online through the PFRDA APY CRA portal (cra-nsdl.com/RiaPRAN) using your PRAN number.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Can I exit the scheme before 60 years?

Voluntary premature exit before 60 is permitted only under exceptional circumstances like terminal illness or death of the subscriber, where the entire corpus is handed to the spouse/nominee.

What happens if there is insufficient balance for auto-debit?

If auto-debit fails, a small penalty (ranging from ₹1 to ₹10 per month) is charged. If contributions stop completely, the account may be deactivated after 6 months and closed after 24 months.

Are premiums tax-deductible?

Yes, contributions made to the Atal Pension Yojana are eligible for tax benefits under Section 80CCD (1B) of the Income Tax Act up to ₹50,000.
